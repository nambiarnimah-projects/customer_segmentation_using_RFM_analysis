# Customer Segmentation using RFM Analysis

## Overview

Understanding customer behavior is essential for businesses that want to make data-driven decisions.
In this project, I explored **RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis** to segment customers based on their purchasing patterns.

Instead of treating all customers equally, RFM analysis helps identify **high-value customers, loyal buyers, and customers who may be at risk of leaving**.

The analysis was performed using **SQL**, and the results were visualized through a **Power BI dashboard**.

---

## Tools Used

* SQL (data preparation and RFM scoring)
* Power BI (data visualization and dashboard design)

---

## Methodology

### 1. Data Exploration

The dataset was first explored and cleaned using SQL.

### 2. RFM Metric Calculation

Three behavioral metrics were calculated:

* **Recency:** Number of days since the customer's last purchase
* **Frequency:** Total number of purchases made by each customer
* **Monetary Value:** Total revenue generated by each customer

### 3. Customer Segmentation

Customers were ranked using SQL window functions and grouped into segments such as:

* Best Customers
* Loyal Customers
* Big Spenders
* Regular Customers
* At Risk Customers
* Lost Customers

These segments help businesses understand which customers drive revenue and which may require engagement strategies.

### 4. Visualization

The final RFM dataset was imported into Power BI to create a dashboard highlighting:

* Customer distribution across segments
* Revenue contribution by segment
* Purchase frequency patterns
* Customer engagement insights

---

## Key Insights

* **Best Customers demonstrated the highest purchase frequency**, indicating strong engagement.
* **Big Spenders contributed the largest share of revenue**, despite lower purchase frequency.
* **At Risk customers still show moderate activity**, suggesting an opportunity for targeted retention strategies.
