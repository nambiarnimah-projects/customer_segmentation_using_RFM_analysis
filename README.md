# E-Commerce Customer Segmentation (RFM Analysis) – SQL + Power BI

##  Project Overview

This project explores customer behavior in an e-commerce dataset using **RFM analysis (Recency, Frequency, Monetary).**

Instead of only analyzing total sales, the goal was to understand how different types of customers interact with the business. By calculating RFM metrics, customers were segmented into groups such as:

- Best Customers  
- Loyal Customers  
- Big Spenders  
- At Risk Customers  
- Lost Customers  

The analysis was performed using **SQL**, and the results were visualized through a **Power BI dashboard** to better understand customer patterns.

This project is part of my ongoing journey learning data analytics and business-focused data analysis.

---

##  Dataset

The dataset contains transaction-level sales data from an e-commerce store, including information about orders, customers, and product purchases.

### Key fields include:
- Order ID  
- Customer ID  
- Order Date  
- Sales amount  
- Product category and sub-category  
- Regional information  

Using this data, customer purchasing behavior was analyzed to calculate RFM metrics.

##  Analysis Objectives

The main objectives of this analysis were:

- Calculate Recency, Frequency, and Monetary (RFM) metrics for each customer  
- Segment customers based on their purchasing behavior  
- Identify high-value customers and potential churn risks  
- Analyze how different customer segments contribute to revenue  
- Build a dashboard to visualize customer behavior and segment performance  

---

##  RFM Methodology

### Recency
Number of days since a customer's most recent purchase.

### Frequency
Total number of purchases made by a customer.

### Monetary Value
Total revenue generated by each customer.

These metrics were scored and combined to classify customers into meaningful segments such as:

- Best Customers  
- Loyal Customers  
- Big Spenders  
- Regular Customers  
- At Risk Customers  
- Lost Customers  

This segmentation helps businesses understand which customers are the most valuable and which may require engagement strategies.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Purchase Frequency
- Best Customers showed the highest average purchase frequency, indicating strong engagement and repeat purchasing behavior.
- Loyal Customers also demonstrated high purchase frequency, making them an important group for maintaining consistent revenue.

### 2. Revenue Contribution
- Big Spenders generated the largest share of total revenue.
- Although they may not purchase as frequently as some other segments, their higher spending per order makes them extremely valuable to the business.

### 3. Customer Retention Opportunities
- At Risk customers still showed moderate purchase activity, suggesting they were previously active buyers who may be starting to disengage.
- This segment represents a potential opportunity for targeted retention strategies.
